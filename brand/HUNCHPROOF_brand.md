## Why the name works

Everyone has a hunch. The problem is a hunch is cheap: you say it, the match ends, and there's no record of whether you were actually right *before the world knew*. Talk is unfalsifiable.

**Hunchproof makes the hunch falsifiable.** You seal your probability call before kickoff (commit-reveal, cryptographically). It's scored against the sharpest forecast that exists — the market's closing line — and only the calls that beat the market *before it moved* accrue to your record. Over time you build something that can't be faked: a proof of foresight.

The name carries the whole thesis in two syllables:
- **hunch** — the human, the read, the gut call. Warm, instant, everyone gets it.
- **proof** — auditable, cryptographic, scored. The thing that separates this from noise.

"Hunch-proof" also reads as *resistant to mere hunches* — the system rewards information, not lucky guesses. That double meaning is the product.

---

## What Hunchproof is (and is not)

**Is:** an auditable market-residual intelligence engine. It measures whether you — and the crowd — contribute real information *beyond* the market's current line, *before* the closing line absorbs it. Two scores forever: absolute calibration (the leaderboard) and market-relative edge (the oracle weight).

**Is not:** a sportsbook or a prediction market. You're not staking money on an outcome. You're proving the quality of your probabilistic foresight against the toughest public benchmark there is. **A belief market, not a money market.**

This distinction is load-bearing — keep it in every public sentence. "Closing line is the bar. CLV is the metric. Commitment binds, reveal verifies."

## Naming layer (decided)

**Single name, top to bottom: Hunchproof** is both the protocol and the product. One word to remember, one word to spread. (A separate protocol-layer name can be introduced later if a B2B oracle business warrants it — not now.)

## Voice notes

- Confident, precise, a little dry — like a sharp quant who also loves football. Never hypey.
- Lead with the human ("your hunch") then earn trust with the rigor ("proof").
- Always draw the betting boundary when describing it publicly — it's a feature, not a disclaimer.
